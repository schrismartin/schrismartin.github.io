Journale | Planned

2016 - Future

With the increasing awareness of mindfullness, many top experts are praising the personal benefits of journaling. Journale aims to do for the personal journal what twitter did for the blog. By encouraging the user to quickly record events in their day in small blurbs, Journale compiles these micro-journals into a private collection of the fragments that makes each day unique.

Daily | Unlisted

December 2015 – Present

While reading David Allen's Getting Things Done, I set out to find the perfect app to implement a strict GTD workflow. Many to-do apps provide exceptional functionality, but do not lock the user into a single methodology. Daily acts to leverage multiple features that make other apps great, while removing extraneous functionality in attempt to lock users in to a strict GTD method.

Available | www.available-app.com

July 2015 – Present

With better event planning in mind, Available acts as a middle-man when it comes to scheduling events between two or more people. Gathering data from your approved calendars, Available creates a semi-public availablity domain that allows your friends to see when you're open to meet. With Smart Compare, Available makes it easy to overlay your availability with multiple users to determine mutually-available times.
